# Having senior developers may not be enough

In more and more organisations, "senior" has become a payroll category, not a skill tier — and AI is exposing the difference at industrial scale.

The familiar story is that juniors are being left behind. The tedious work that used to build their pattern recognition — debugging, tracing, reading ten-year-old code — now takes minutes in a model, and they never get the reps. That is real. But it is only half the picture.

The other half is that in many organisations, the seniors mentoring them don't have the taste either. Fifteen years in the same codebase produces something real — knowledge of where the bodies are buried — but it is the expertise of a curator, not an architect. It tells you what not to break. It does not tell you what to build. And when an AI hands them a plausible-looking service with subtly wrong coupling, they sign off, because it looks like what they would have written.

This is the uncomfortable pattern: AI does not cause quality problems. It amplifies whatever quality culture an organisation already has. Teams with real craft — people who read widely, refactor continuously, argue productively about design — ship better, faster, cleaner systems. Teams without it produce slop at a scale nobody has seen before, wrapped in a veneer of professionalism that makes structural rot harder to spot.

It was always an organisational problem. Deming's central insight — that quality is a system output, and systems are management's responsibility — is about to become impossible to ignore in software. You cannot hire your way out of a taste deficit when your own "senior" benchmarks are themselves part of the deficit.

The only way through is for upper management to develop enough literacy to distinguish craft from cargo-culting, the humility to defer to those who have it, and the political spine to protect quality investments whose payoff comes after the next earnings call.

The juniors will not save you. Neither will the AI. The lever is, and always was, further up.
