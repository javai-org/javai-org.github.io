## Outline

Challenge the reader to distinguish "software developer" from "software engineer". Acknowledge that the reader perhaps sees no obvious difference — perhaps it is just personal preference. Then modestly suggest that, if we choose to, we can create a meaningful and useful distinction between the two:

- **The software developer** creates software, writes tests, deploys, and can make no empirically backed statement as to how the software will perform in the presence of uncertainty — which any non-trivial software encounters daily.
- **The software engineer**, by contrast, has proactively estimated how their software will perform under uncertain conditions or in the face of non-determinacy, and can offer a measure of confidence in that performance.

## Closer

Are you a software developer or a software engineer? Think about that the next time you apply for a job.

One line to pin to the wall above your desk:

> **Don't ship application behaviour that you haven't quantified.**
